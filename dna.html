Deoxyribonucleic Acid

DNA is the hereditary material in humans and almost all other organisms. It contains the instructions needed for an organism to develop, survive, and reproduce.

Chemical Bases

Adenine (A)
Thymine (T)
Guanine (G)
Cytosine (C)

Hydrogen Bonding

Base pairs are held together by hydrogen bonds. G-C pairs are harder to break because they have more bonds.

A = T : 2 Hydrogen Bonds
G ≡ C : 3 Hydrogen Bonds

Structure

The double helix features a sugar-phosphate backbone and two antiparallel strands rotating around a common axis.
